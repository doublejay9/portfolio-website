Problem Tenants & The Process of Eviction

If you own rental property in Bardford, Maryland, Wooddew Property Management can help you handle problem tenants and the process of eviction. Every landlord will have to deal with problem tenants throughout their career. Knowing when to be firm and when it's best to be flexible can be hard for first-timers to judge, and experienced landlords can get worn down from the stress, particularly when they own multiple properties with multiple families each. Having professional property managers on your side who have seen it before can help salvage positive relationships without sacrificing your business.

Common Landlord Problems

Some of the issues listed below are more common in multi-unit apartment buildings than single-family homes. Regardless of the kind of property you own, these are the problems every landlord will likely face.

  • Late and partial rent payments
  • Chronic complaining
  • Frequently hosting unexpected guests
  • Noise
  • Property damage (by people and pets)
  • Parking issues
  • Subletting
  • Criminal activity

How People Become Problem Tenants

From a landlord's perspective, human beings are diverse in frustrating ways. Some people excel at annoying others, from the apparent criminals to the relatively harmless chronic complainers.

Good people can fall on hard times. They aren't making the money they used to, so rent payments come in late. In their desperation, they may start subletting without checking to make sure it's okay (or even legal in your area). Reasonable people can also crack and do strange things on a whim, like paint the walls lime green and adopt a parakeet in spite of your strict no-pets policy.

The takeaway here is that, while there will always be bad eggs, being a bad tenant doesn't necessarily make someone a bad person. You need to be firm for the sake of your business. However, being transparent, polite, and offering your tenants options when possible will go a long way to keeping the good eggs happy and in your rental units.
