Three Areas of Property Management

You can divide the work that goes into managing rental properties into three areas: the property itself, your tenants, and your business finances. Since you own the investment property, it is your responsibility to keep it safe, well-maintained, and a place your tenants will want to call home. Managing tenants is managing people, so a landlord must have the skills to handle tenants' emotions (like anger) professionally, motivate neglectful ones, and confront those who are behaving badly. Problems with the property and with tenants are the most obvious, painful, and pressing, so first-time landlords will find plenty of advice on them.

Managing finances isn't discussed nearly as often as the other two areas of property management. Frequent tenant turnover and extended vacancies hurt your earning potential, and what you do earn gets swallowed up the moment your property needs costly repairs. Bardford real estate agents can help you fill vacancies, and scheduling regular inspections and repairs with trusted vendors can help minimize the impact on your bottom line. Still, it's up to you (and your accountant) to make sure your expenses don't send your business into debt.

Property Management Tips

Ask a dozen Bardford real estate experts, and you'll get a dozen different property management strategies. Each rental market has its quirks. What works in one neighborhood won't work in another. All that said, there are several things any landlord can do to keep tenants happy and stay profitable.

  • Familiarize yourself with the Fair Housing Act and Maryland landlord-tenant laws.
  • When screening potential tenants, perform credit, criminal, and eviction history checks to get a complete picture.
  • Accept online rent payments to make it easy for tenants to pay on time.
  • Respond to and follow up on tenant complaints in a timely fashion.
  • Perform regular property inspections (every 3 to 6 months) to catch problems before they become emergencies.
  • Make strategic upgrades to appliances and fixtures to keep the property attractive to new tenants.
  • Hire a property manager to handle the day-to-day tasks for you.
