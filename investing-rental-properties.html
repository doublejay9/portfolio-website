What Type of Property is Right for a Beginner?

If you're new to real estate investing, starting small -- a single apartment, duplex, or condo -- is best for getting comfortable with the process and your new responsibilities. Unless you're skilled in construction, avoid buildings that need extensive renovations and focus on properties that only need minor repairs. Beyond these basic guidelines, you should identify the kinds of people you want to attract as buyers or tenants and choose rental properties that meet their needs. Our local knowledge of the Bardford area can help you find the right place in the perfect neighborhood for your future buyers or tenants.

How Much Should You Pay?

Always buy below market value to maximize the return on your investment. Most experts recommend buying at 70% market value or less. Keep in mind that investment properties come with higher down payments (at least 20%) and higher property taxes. That's in addition to renovation and operating costs.
